Robotic Cancer Surgery

In Robotic Cancer Surgery, robotic techniques enable a radical operation to be performed with preservation of nerves and other critical structures, due to better visualisation and articulating instruments bringing more precision and lesser side effect.

The surgical treatment for cancers has traditionally been done by open techniques. An open surgery is associated with a large wound, greater pain & delayed recovery and return to normal activities.


Read More

Laparoscopic Cancer Surgery

Laparoscopic Cancer Surgery or "Minimally Invasive" surgery is a specialized technique for performing surgery using small incisions of 0.5 to 1cm in size as a result of which there is less pain and early recovery.

Laparoscopic surgery uses around 4 to 5 small incisions of 0.5-1cm in size. Each incision is called a "Port".


Read More

Head & Neck Reconstructive Surgery

Head and neck cancers are a significant problem in our country constituting approximately one-third of all cancer cases. This surgery is to eradicate the cancerous lesion while preserving the look and function of the affected organ as much as possible.

Head and neck reconstruction surgery (Plastic Surgery) is performed to replace missing tissue with pieces of tissue from other parts of the body. This could be in form of a skin graft, a pedicled flap or a free flap.

Oncoplastic Breast Surgery

Breast cancer is the commonest cancer affecting women. In india more than 80,000 new breast cancers are diagnosed every year. Although it affects women of all ages, more commonly seen in postmenopausal women above 55 years.

Breast cancer is the most common cause of cancer-related deaths in women. It is curable if detected in early stages.

Heated Intraperitoneal Chemotherapy (HIPEC)

Hyper Thermic Intraperitoneal Chemotherapy (HIPEC) is a highly concentrated, heated chemotherapy treatment that is delivered directly to the abdomen during surgery. It is used for treating cancer arising from ovary, appendix, colon etc.

Traditionally gastrointestinal cancers which had spread to the abdominal cavity lining (i.e. Peritoneum) were treated with a nihilistic attitude and were considered stage 4. Their treatment has been similar to those of other metastatic solid tumors which include intravenous chemotherapy.

About Dr. Aditya Manke

Dr. Aditya Manke is a renowned specialist in Robotic and Laparoscopic Onco Surgery. With several prestigious degrees and fellowships from the UK, Dr. Manke is an expert in advanced cancer surgeries, including HIPEC and Robotic Oncosurgery. He is dedicated to providing patients with the highest level of care and surgical excellence.

MBBS and MS from the prestigious Grant Medical College and Sir J J Group of Hospitals at Mumbai. Dr. Aditya Manke has completed fellowship in Surgical Oncology at Tata Medical Centre, Kolkata with a special focus on Gastrointestinal Cancers. Later he was trained at The Christie NHS Manchester UK in Robotic cancer surgery & Cytoreductive Surgery with HIPEC followed by a year of training at Sir Gangaram Hospital, New Delhi. He also earned the prestigious MRCS & FRCS from Royal College of Surgeons Edinburgh, UK, and Fellowship of European Board of Surgery in Surgical Oncology (FEBS) at Brussels in 2016.
